3          CASE MANAGEMENT ENCOUNTER (CME)

 

The following functions are part of the Case Management Encounter, found in the (E) beside the clients name in the OSCAR Medical view.

 

3.1      Case Notes and Issues

 

Case notes are organized by issues, for example, if the worker feels that addictions are an issue for a client, that issue will be the heading for which all resulting case notes are attached. This system of organization allows for case notes to be sorted by issues. For example if a worker wants to view all case notes on Addictions or Housing, they would select that issue and all the case notes pertaining to that issue would be filtered out.

To Write a New Note:

-          To access the case notes you need to select “E” beside a client’s name in the Oscar Medical List  

-          A new screen will open up. This brings you to the case management encounter    (CME)– where the case notes are written

-    Click “New Note”

-    Follow the sequence of events laid out on the page: start with selecting an issue

-          If the issue you need is not there – select “Add an Issue” – enter in the issue you wish to search for into search engine (ex. schizophrenia in search engine – will bring up list of related issues)

-          If you are looking for a counselor related issue type CTCMM in the search                  engine (City of Toronto Case Management Module) – all counselor related issues will appear

-          Click the box (s) on the left hand side beside the issue or issues you would like to add to your list

-          Once you have selected the issue(s) you would like to write your note about click “Add Checked Issue” at the bottom of the screen.

-          Click on the issue(s) you would like to write your note about.

-          Then begin writing your note and spell check if you like.

-          Clarify what kind of encounter you had (i.e. face to face, phone, without client)

-          Check the box beside “Sign” – this completes your note

-          Select “Save and Exit” or save if you want to keep the screen open

 

Notes can be filtered by DATE, PROVIDER, PROGRAM, and ROLE.

 For example:

Once in the CASE MANAGEMENT ENCOUNTER you will see to the right under progress notes an option to ‘SORT’.  Now you can click on the drop down menu and filter your notes by either DATE, PROVIDER, PROGRAM, or ROLE.  You can also click on the name of the provider you are looking for. 

 

3.2      Editing Case Notes and Note History

 

By making edits to a note, the history of the note edits is maintained and can be reviewed by anyone. The original note can never be deleted or permanently altered – edits can be made but the original note is always the there. The edits will act as addendums.

-  All signed and saved notes will have a PEN and PENCIL icon beside them

-  Click on the “pen and pencil” icon which will allow you edit the note

-  Make any relevant edits.

-  Click “sign”

-  Click “save and exit”

-  Now a CLOCK icon with an arrow will appear beside the PEN AND PENCIL icon in the Case Management Encounter. 

-  Click on the “clock icon” and you will now be able to see the history of that note –   note that the original note is present, the edits are additions

-  Note: all edits are time/date/user stamped

-  Click "close this page” once you are done viewing the history of the note. 

 

3.3      Locking and Unlocking Case Notes

 

The CME function permits a user to lock a case note(s). This means that the note will become accessible by those who know the password. 

 

-  Write a case note as you normally would

 

-  Select "encounter type"

 

-  Select "Sign"

 

-  Note that at the bottom of the case note the option to enter a password before it is

   saved.

 

-  Enter a password chosen by yourself and/or your team for example "1234"  or "apple"

 

-  Select "Save and Exit"

 

-  You will be back in the CASE MANAGEMENT ENCOUNTER and you will notice a

    LOCK icon beside the note you just wrote.

 

-  The status of your note will say SIGNED/ LOCKED.

 

-  Only those with rights to the password can unlock the note

 

-  Notes are only unlocked during the session that a provider is logged in – the

   password will have to be re-entered to access that note if the provider logs out and

   logs back in

 

-  The password can be changed at anytime by someone who knows the original

    password

 

-  To permanently unlock the note you can go back into the case note by clicking on "edit note" which is the PEN AND PENCIL ICON.

 

-  Scroll to the bottom of the case note and delete the password entered in the

   password field.

 

-  Click "Save and Exit"
